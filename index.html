Essential Preventive Measures

Simple, effective actions that significantly reduce the spread of infectious diseases

Hand Hygiene

When: Before eating, after using restroom, after coughing/sneezing, when hands are visibly dirty

How: Wash with soap and water for 20+ seconds, or use hand sanitizer with 60%+ alcohol content

Impact: Reduces transmission of respiratory and gastrointestinal infections by up to 50%

Respiratory Protection

When: In crowded indoor spaces, healthcare settings, when ill, during outbreaks

How: Wear well-fitting masks covering nose and mouth, use N95/KN95 for high-risk situations

Impact: Significantly reduces airborne disease transmission, protects both wearer and others

Physical Distancing

When: During outbreaks, in crowded spaces, when someone is symptomatic

How: Maintain 6 feet distance, avoid crowds, choose outdoor activities when possible

Impact: Reduces exposure to respiratory droplets and airborne pathogens

Environmental Hygiene

When: Daily cleaning routine, after illness, in shared spaces

How: Disinfect frequently-touched surfaces, maintain clean living/working spaces

Impact: Eliminates pathogens from surfaces, prevents indirect transmission

Adequate Ventilation

When: In indoor spaces, especially during gatherings

How: Open windows, use air purifiers, ensure HVAC systems are properly maintained

Impact: Dilutes airborne pathogens, reduces concentration of infectious particles

Isolation When Ill

When: Experiencing symptoms, diagnosed with infectious disease

How: Stay home, separate from household members, seek medical care as needed

Impact: Prevents community spread, protects vulnerable populations

Key Vaccination Facts

  • Vaccines prevent 2-3 million deaths annually worldwide
  • Childhood vaccination programs have reduced vaccine-preventable disease deaths by over 99%
  • COVID-19 vaccines prevented millions of hospitalizations and deaths
  • Influenza vaccination reduces flu risk by 40-60% when well-matched to circulating viruses
  • HPV vaccination can prevent 90% of cervical cancers
